A note on what we are building. Coming soon

An assistant that lives on your computer.

Not on someone else's.

Right now, talking to AI means talking to a server somewhere far away — logged, metered, and never quite yours.

We don't think it has to be that way.

Your conversations
belong to you.

GreenCube is a desktop app. The model runs on your CPU and GPU, the messages sit on your hard drive, and the network never has to be involved. We can't read what you write to it. Nobody can.

Today's open models are
sharper than last year's secrets.

The good news is the model is no longer the bottleneck. A 5.7 GB file running locally now matches what last year's frontier models did over the wire — for code, for writing, for thinking out loud. You don't need the cloud anymore. You just need the file.

It picks the right model.
You just open it.

On first launch GreenCube reads your hardware — how much RAM you have, what GPU you're running — and downloads the best open model that fits. No settings to tune. No tier to choose. Open the app. Start writing.

Particulars
Platform
Windows. macOS and Linux to follow.
Memory
8 GB and up
Storage
About 6 GB on disk
Network
Only to download the model. Then never again.
Models
Phi-4 Mini, Qwen3.5, Gemma 4 — all open weights
How it works
  1. Download GreenCube. One installer. About 60 MB before any model is fetched.
  2. It picks your model. Based on your RAM and GPU. The first launch pulls the file; after that you're offline forever if you want.
  3. Open it. Write. Code, draft, think. No accounts, no quotas, no pings home.

GreenCube is being built quietly, by a small team that thinks the future of personal AI shouldn't be rented from anyone. We're getting close.
